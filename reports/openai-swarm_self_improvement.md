## RESEARCH FIELD OVERVIEW
Self-evolving agents represent a transformative shift in artificial intelligence, emphasizing systems that can autonomously improve their capabilities without human intervention. These agents are particularly significant in environments that require dynamic adaptability and continuous learning, such as complex decision-making tasks, wireless communication systems, and domain-specific applications like biomedical research. The research explores methods for integrating advanced meta-learning, self-reflection, and knowledge distillation techniques, enabling agents to address intricate challenges beyond the reach of static AI models. The primary objective is to foster agents that excel in general-purpose applications, providing consistent performance improvements and reducing the need for costly human oversight.

Furthermore, the domain is witnessing a merging of methodology-centric and data-centric innovations, which are crucial in large-scale applications and scenarios where environmental variables are inherently unpredictable. This cross-pollination of techniques not only supports more robust and adaptable agentic designs but also pushes the boundaries of AI capabilities in real-world systems.
